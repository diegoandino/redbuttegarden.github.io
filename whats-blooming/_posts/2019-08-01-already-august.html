It's already August, but you're in luck, there's still summertime left... so don't delay visiting the Garden any longer! Following is a hint of what's happening in the Rose Garden.


Don Sudbury Daylily     Hemerocallis 'Sudbury Don'

This Daylily, named after the late Don Sudbury, who along with his wife Beverly, helped create the Volunteer Program here at Red Butte Garden over 30 years ago.


Bev Sudbury Lily     Hemerocallis 'Sudbury Bev'

Named after the late Beverly Sudbury. The Sudbury Daylilies can be seen next to the benches dedicated to the late Sudbury couple.


Blue Satin® Rose of Sharon     Hibiscus syriacus 'Marina'

These vibrant flowers bloom from late summer until frost.


Panicle Hydrangea     Hydrangea paniculata 'Tardiva'

Showy white blooms turn purplish-pink with age on this vigorous, upright, and fast growing cultivar.


Snow Queen Oakleaf Hydrange     Hydrangea quercifolia 'Snow Queen'

Produces 8-inch-long conical flower heads beginning in early summer.


Lyda Rose     Rosa 'LETlyda'

This Rose is a lightly fragranced and a prolific bloomer from summer into fall.


Don't let summer pass you by without visiting the Garden!
