There is only one week left of June and it certainly shows here in the Garden. There is an abundance of blooms as well as a large Lego display throughout the Garden. Come see it for yourself!


Showy Milkweed     Asclepias speciosa

This statue in the background portrays the importance of Milkweed to the survival of the Monarch Butterfly (Danaus plexippus). You can see both the real plant and the Lego version up close and personal in the Wildflower Meadow!


Brakelights® Red Yucca     Hesperaloe parviflora 'Perpa'

What beautiful bright flowers this plant displays! It can be seen at the bottom of the Water Conservation Garden.


Velvet Cloak Smokebush     Cotinus coggygria 'Velvet Cloak'

This picture does not do this large shrub justice. It is showing off right now in the Four Seasons Lawn.


Oriental Poppy     Papaver 'Princess Victoria Louise'

Not only were the flowers delightful, but now is the fruits time to shine. It can be seen at the bottom of the Water Conservation Garden.


Praying Mantis Lego Statue

It's a giant praying mantis! You can see it along the Floral Walk.


Inca Ice TM Peruvian Lily    Alstroemeria 'Koice'

It is always a joy when this lily starts to bloom. View it on the Floral Walk.


Come give June a warm sendoff by seeing what it brought to the Garden! Don'f forget your sunblock!
