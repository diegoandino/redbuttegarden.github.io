Things are waking up all over the Garden. The early spring bulbs are finishing up and the Daffodils are just beginning to put on a show.

Rip Van Winkle Daffodil   Narcissus 'Rip Van Winkle'

You can find this double-flowered Daffodil scattered throughout the Rose Garden.


Pheasant's Eye Daffodil   Narcissus poeticus

Keep your eyes open for this beauty along the woodland paths on the Floral Walk.


Golden Lotus Hellebore   Helleborus 'Golden Lotus'

While exploring the woodland paths, watch for a colorful display of Hellebores under the Amelanchier trees.


Hardy Cyclamen   Cyclamen coum

If planted in well draining soil, Hardy Cyclamens can bloom as early as January!


Featherleaf Kittentails   Synthyris pinnatifida

This native alpine beauty can be found growing on the Sprout House roof in the Children's Garden.


Oxlip   Primula elatior

As you enjoy the Water Conservation Garden, look for this beauty around the pond in the Rain Garden.


Alpine Dog Violet   Viola labradorica

Alpine Dog Violet is a flower you will often smell long before you see it. Look for this fragrant beauty in the Medicinal and Children's Gardens.


Come visit the Garden soon and watch as the Daffodils transform the landscape into a sea of yellow.
