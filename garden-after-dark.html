FAQ

Do adults need to purchase Garden After Dark tickets?
Yes. Everyone over the age of 2 is required to have a ticket to enter the event. Don’t forget children two and under are free!


Can I purchase tickets ahead of time?
You can pre-purchase tickets online below or by phone at (801) 581-8454. Tickets can be picked up at Will Call on the night of the event. Be sure to bring a valid form of ID when you pick up your tickets.


What is your refund policy?
All ticket sales are final. No refunds, replacements, or exchanges.


Are admission tickets or passes to the Garden the same as tickets for Garden After Dark?
No. Garden After Dark is a special event and requires a different ticket than general admission to the Garden. Passes such as the Connect Pass or Wasatch Savings Card are only good for discounted or free general admission to the Garden - they are NOT valid toward the purchase of Garden After Dark tickets or other special events.


What should I bring with me to the event?
Garden After Dark includes both indoor and outdoor activities so dress for the weather! Garden paths will be lit during the event, but you may also want to bring a flashlight to help guide your way.


Can I wear a costume?
Yes! Dress your family in their Halloween best! Costumes are encouraged but please make sure they conform to our NEW Costume Policy.


What’s included in the price of Garden After Dark admission?
All activity stations located throughout the Garden are included with admission. The only items not included in admission are goods sold by food vendors, craft vendors, and the Red Butte Garden Gift Shop.


What activities will be available this year?
Full descriptions of this year’s activity stations can be found below.


Where do I park?
Guest are encouraged to park in the University of Utah parking lots surrounding the Amphitheatre (see map). Guests are welcome to drop off passengers at the Amphitheatre gates before parking.

Calling all witches and wizards! Join us for an enchanted evening at the fabled Oaklore Adademy of Magic, the premier school of witchcraft and wizardry. Explore the seemingly magical properties of real-lie plants from around the world as you select a magic wand, dig into herology, study magical creatures, and more! But be warned, the noxious witch Myrtle Spurge is at Oaklore creating mischief and mayhem. Can you complete your magical education and collect the magic spell ingredients you need to banish Myrtle Spurge from Oaklore? Find out this October at Garden After Dark!

With crafts, activities, light displays, games, and more, this is a fun-filled evening you and your family won’t want to miss! Join us for Garden After Dark, an indoor/outdoor, costume and kid-friendly experience in the Garden to celebrate Halloween.

NEW! We have expanded Garden After Dark this year. The main entry to the event will be through the Red Butte Garden Amphitheatre gates, and not at the Visitor Center. Please park near the Amphitheatre.

PROLOGUE

CLASS: WAND THEORY 101
Professor Walter Cottom

Does the wand choose the wizard or the wizard choose the wand? Under the direction of Professor Cottam, you’ll explore the art of wand making and learn a few magical qualities of trees in the real world. Remember to keep an eye out for Myrtle Spurge! She was last seen near the Wand Theory classroom, and it looks like she was carrying a large stack of student wands!


CLASS: POTIONS LAB 202
Professor Gertrude Jekyll

From wolfsbane to elder berries, plants are a common ingredient in most potions at Oaklore. Under Professor Jekyll’s guidance, you’ll explore the variety of plants used to make potions, lotions, and tinctures from around the world. But it looks like Myrtle Spurge is up to her old tricks and has hidden all the potion ingredients in a mystical maze. Can you help Professor Jekyll find the ingredients you need to brew today’s potion?


CLASS: CHARMS 303
Professor Beatrix Potter

Wands at the ready! All witches and wizards need to know a spell or two, and no one teaches them better than Professor Potter. With her help, you’ll learn a variety of botanically-inspired spells that might prove quite useful in helping to capture Myrtle Spurge.


CLASS: MAGICAL CREATURES STUDIES 404
Professor Charles Darwin

Magical creatures abound in the natural world, and Professor Darwin is an expert cryptozoologist (someone who studies magical creatures). With his help, you’ll study a small selection of magical creatures who help care for and protect the natural world. But it looks like Myrtle Spurge has been here and released all of Professor Darwin’s creatures! Can you help him find them before they damage the school?



CLASS: HERBOLOGY 505
Professor Ellen Hutchins

Professor Hutchins is a world-renowned herbologist on sabbatical from a famous school of witchcraft and wizardry in England. With her help, you’ll dive into the world of magic plants, and learn about one special plant that doesn’t need soil to grow. But it looks like Myrtle Spurge has one last trick up her sleeve! She’s demolished Professor Hutchins’s magic plant specimens. Can you help Professor Hutchins fix them?


FINAL EXAM
Professor Chauncy, Headmaster of Oaklore Academy

Myrtle Spurge has caused quite enough mischief for one night. Can you help banish her from Oaklore Academy? Using everything you’ve learned, put your knowledge to the test in one final challenge and see if you can expel Myrtle Spurge from school.
