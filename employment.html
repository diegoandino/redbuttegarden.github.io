ASSISTANT LANDSCAPE DESIGNER

Administration Department
Full time, benefitted position

Assistant Landscape Designer

Administration Department
Full time, benefitted position

Salary

$12.02 - $19.00 an hour

Schedule

40 hours per week. Monday - Friday; 8:30 am to 5 pm; occasional evening and weekend

Job Summary

Red Butte Garden is looking for a Assistant Landscape Designer to work with the Landscape Architect in multiple aspects of Red Butte Garden projects, including product and construction research, materials takeoffs, quote requests from vendors, cost estimating, design plans and detailing, graphic presentations, bid documents, and construction observation.

This is a full time benefited position with funding for two years, may be extended depended on continued support.

Responsibilities

  • 1. Assists Landscape Architect and Red Butte Garden personnel as directed in developing and executing planning, design and construction project requirements.
  • 2. Prepares visual simulations and graphic presentations of proposed improvements and construction.
  • 3. Prepares draft and final graphic art documents for production of signs and plaques.
  • 4. Provides research of construction methods, materials and costs.
  • 5. Performs material takeoffs, makes quote requests and prepares labor and material estimates.
  • 6. Assists in organization and maintenance of project files.
  • 7. Integrates data from multiple drawing/graphic formats (pdf, dwg, jpg) to create plans and maps of Garden infrastructure.
  • 8. Develops construction plans and details for contractor estimates and implementation on small landscape projects throughout Red Butte Garden.
  • 9. Assists in review of plans and specifications for construction projects.
  • 10. Assists with construction observation and inspections.

Qualifications

Associate’s degree in Architectural Drafting or equivalency; one year of previous architectural drafting experience using computer aided design techniques; a working knowledge of building construction and remodeling methods and materials; and excellent written communication skills, oral communication skills and interpersonal skills required. Demonstrated customer service skills to deal effectively with others are also required.

Preferences

Bachelor’s degree in Landscape Architecture or related field of study, plus 1-2 years of professional design experience. Demonstrated ability to read blueprints and building diagrams. Demonstrated proficiency and ability to create and modify documents with AutoCAD, Adobe products (including Illustrator, Photoshop and InDesign), Word and Excel. GIS experience preferred.

Demonstrated human relation and effective written and verbal communication skills are required.

Job Summary

Red Butte Garden is seeking a Youth & Family Special Events Assistant helps plan, create, and coordinate Red Butte Garden’s Youth & Family special events that advance the Garden’s mission to connect people with plants and the beauty of living landscapes.

This job might be for you if:

  • You enjoy problem-solving, taking on difficult challenges, and finding creative solutions. If you don’t know the answer, you’ll dig until you discover it.
  • You don’t get flustered easily, and are often described as patient, level-headed, and cool under pressure.
  • You like teaching people and get a kick out of helping them reach those aha! moments. Teaching someone something new makes you feel warm and fuzzy inside.
  • You pay attention to the details. As far as you’re concerned, anything worth doing is worth doing right, every single time. You stay focused, and nothing falls through the cracks on your watch.
  • You think on your feet. You like learning new things and tend to learn quickly. When things change, you know how to roll with the punches.
  • You are motivated and driven. You volunteer for new challenges without waiting to be asked. You’re going to take ownership of the time you spend with us and truly make a difference.
  • You not only love listening to ideas, questions, and critiques from teammates with grace and patience, but you can also eloquently and logically explain your thoughts and programming decisions. You see this type of exchange as critical to the success of yourself and our programs: it is through sharing ideas and being open to the brilliance of others that we arrive at the best solutions.

Responsibilities

  • Your main responsibilities include helping to plan, create, and coordinate the Garden’s upcoming exhibit, Nature Connects: Art with LEGO Bricks. You’ll help ensure the run of the exhibit is smooth and executed to the Garden’s standards of excellence by creating new programs, leading public and private tours, assisting with exhibit interpretation, daily maintenance, and more.
  • You’ll also assist with other Youth & Family Programs including Arbor Day, Monday Family Nights, Garden After Dark, Summer Camps, and more.
  • You’ll shepherd new programs from ideation to release and work with others to refine and evaluate the end result.
  • You’ll assist our seasonal education staff and volunteers by providing direction, leadership, and feedback.
  • You’ll perform any other departmental duties as needed and lend a helping hand to other departments at the discretion of the Youth & Family Programs Manager and the Youth & Family Programs Assistant Manager.

High school diploma or equivalency required. One year full-time customer service experience or equivalency, demonstrated human relation and effective communication skills also required.

Applicants must demonstrate the potential ability to perform the essential functions of the job as outlined in the position description.

Preferences

  • You have experience working in special events, education, environmental studies, or horticulture.
  • You’re passionate about the environment and sharing your passion with others.
  • You communicate clearly and can explain just about anything to anyone. You’re comfortable communicating in writing and in person.
  • You enjoy working with others in a team but are also able to work independently and produce stellar results.
  • An interest in plants demonstrated either by previous employment or education.
  • You’re enthusiastic about learning and education.
  • You’re dependable, punctual, and patient
