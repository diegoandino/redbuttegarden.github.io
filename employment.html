EMPLOYMENT


Red Butte Garden seeks to connect people with plants and the beauty of living landscapes. Our staff is a community that understands, values, protects, and is enriched by the world of plants.

Whether you are working outside with our awesome Horticulture staff, or helping the garden grow behind the scenes, you will get to work in a beautiful environment with the best people around.

If you love nature as much as we do and want to be part of an awesome team, apply today!

Youth Programs Coordinator

Programs Department
Full time, benefitted position

Salary

$12.02 to $17.00 an hour

Schedule

40 hours per week, M-F 8:30 am to 5 pm; some evenings and weekends required.

Job Summary

Red Butte Garden is seeking a Youth Education Coordinator. Under the direction of the Youth and Family Programs Manager, the Youth Education Coordinator is responsible for administering, developing, promoting, and evaluating youth education programs for Red Butte Garden.

Responsibilities

  • Develop and oversee RBG’s on-site Youth Programs including, but not limited to: Summer Camps, Garden Adventure classes, Lil’ Buds classes, and Sprout House Workshops
  • Create interpretive content for Sprout House and Children’s Garden
  • Identify and develop partnerships with other organizations to extendRBGeducational programming.
  • Assist with planning, preparation, and execution of other Youth and Family events including Arbor Day, Summer Campouts, Monday Family Nights, Garden After Dark, Winter Solstice, Learning Labs, and Traveling Exhibits
  • Assist with hiring, managing, training, mentoring, and supervising Youth and Family Programs Staff
  • Assist with purchasing, managing program budgets, and maintaining supplies and materials for all Youth and Family Programs
  • Responsible for organization of appropriate program assessment, evaluation, and management of Youth and Family Programs volunteers.
  • Perform any other departmental duties as directed by Youth and Family Programs Manager and/or Programs Director

Qualifications

Associate’s degree in a related field, or equivalency, plus two years of related experience required; ability to use logic and reasoning to prioritize tasks; computer skills including word processing, spreadsheet software, and/or database software preferred; demonstrated human relation and effective communication skills also required.

Bachelor’s degree in a related field may be preferred.

Applicants must demonstrate the potential ability to perform the essential functions of the job as outlined in the position description.

Preferences

  • Bachelor’s degree in a related area or equivalency (2 years related work experience may be substituted for 1 year of education), and two years related experience preferred
  • Bachelor’s degree in horticulture, botany, environmental studies, education, or a related field that includes plant-based coursework
  • Demonstrated interest in and commitment to botany/horticulture, natural ecosystems, and water conservation
  • One year of teaching experience with children in a school, museum, or camp setting
  • Demonstrated human relations and effective communication skills
  • Experience developing and evaluating curriculum
  • Proven written and verbal communication skills
  • Must be flexible and able to work both independently and as part of a team
  • Demonstrated ability to analyze and make decisions related to program content
  • Established management and administrative skills
  • Applicants must demonstrate the potential ability to perform the essential functions of the job as outlined in the position description.

GREENHOUSE GARDENER

Horticulture Department
Part time, non-benefitted position (seasonal)

Greenhouse Gardener

Horticulture Department
Part time, non-benefitted position (seasonal)

Salary

$10.50 - $11.00 an hour

Schedule

19 hrs per week - work schedule varies, weekend shift required

Job Summary

The Horticulture Department at Red Butte Garden is looking for a part-time gardener for our greenhouse team. We are seeking someone who wants to work with and learn more about plants and gardening, and enjoys working both indoors in a greenhouse and working outdoors. Would you enjoy learning more about gardening, and working with people who are passionate about plants and nature? We strongly prefer people who are committed to the role of a Botanical Garden and want to be a part of a great team!

Responsibilities

  • A primary duty is to ensure through detail oriented hand watering that plants have the proper moisture levels.
  • Must be willing and able to perform detail hand watering for several hours a day; co-lift 60 lb bales of soil/media and large plants for interior-scape changeouts; operate soil mixer; work from ladders to hang shade cloths, change lights, hang/maintain color baskets; use a backpack sprayer to apply sanitation and disinfectant products and pesticides like insecticidal soaps and horticultural oils, under the supervision of a certified pesticide applicator; flexible with working on a variety of projects associated with simple greenhouse facilities maintenance, cleaning, and crop production.
  • Other duties include: make hose repairs as needed, weeding, planting, deadheading, pruning, fertilization, transplanting, sweeping, & event support.

Qualifications

Nine months professional work experience with the responsibility for five or more acres of grounds, or equivalency; a valid Utah vehicle operator’s license required; and demonstrated human relations and effective communication skills required.

Preferences

  • Nine months professional work experience or education in a related field preferred, but not required.
  • Must be able to work 16- 19/hrs week, year-round.
  • Must be able to work outdoors in a variety of weather conditions and temperature extremes, and understand the physical commitment required for gardening.
  • Must have proven written and oral communication skills, a commitment to provide excellent customer service and ability to work cooperatively with others.
