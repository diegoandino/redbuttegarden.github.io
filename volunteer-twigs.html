Internship Summary

TWIG volunteers work alongside adult instructors and volunteers to help lead garden-based activities for children ages 5-12 in our Summer Camp Program or joining one of our Horticulture crews for hands-on experience. TWIGs spend most of their time outdoors, behind-the-scenes with Red Butte Garden staff, Summer Camp kids, and Garden guests. Additionally, TWIGs learn how to be environmental educators as they gain experience planning and teaching youth programs on a variety of subjects including but not limited to: botany, horticulture, environmentalism, and more. TWIGs will also gain valuable job skills including teamwork, communication, public speaking, and responsibility.

Responsibilities

Internship duties include but are not limited to:

  • Helping lead instructor prepare daily camp materials, or helping crew leads prepare for shifts
  • Assisting lead instructor and volunteers with classroom management
  • Developing, planning, preparing, and teaching summer camp activities as directed by lead instructor
  • Assisting with daily operations by maintaining supplies, facilities, and equipment

Qualifications

  • Open to high school students entering Grades 9-12 (ages 14-17, must be age 14 before your first shift)
  • Proven written and verbal communication skills
  • An interest in plants, environmental education, and/or working with children
  • Must be flexible and able to work both independently and as part of a team

Other Information

  • TWIG Internships run June 5 through August 11, 2017
  • Summer Camp runs June 12 through August 11, 2017
  • Summer Camp Shifts Available: Morning (8:30AM-12:30PM) or Afternoon (12:30PM-4:30PM)
  • Summer Camp is a full two-week commitment
  • Both Horticulture and Education applicants must be available and dedicated to committing to three required monthly leadership trainings
  • Horticulture crew TWIGs must complete one three-hour shift every week for seven weeks (Mon-Thurs 9AM-Noon)
