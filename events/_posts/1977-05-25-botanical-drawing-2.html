REGISTRATION REQUIRED


MONDAYS, JUNE 6-JULY 11 @ 6-9PM

NO CLASS JULY 4th



This class is a continuation of Botanical Drawing 1. Students will refine their observational drawing skills through careful observation of plant structures. Students will learn to accurately draw botanical forms using pencil as the primary medium. Emphasis will be placed on line, form, light & shade, and simple perspective.


MATERIALS LIST

  • Graphite Pencils: 6H, 4H, 2H, HB, 2B, 4B, 6B Brand of your choice. Note: Tom Bow pencils available only online.

  • Pad of drawing paper 11x14. Strathmore is available at Utrecht/Blick in Sugarhouse and Michaels. 300 or 400 series. This paper is acid free

  • Pad of tracing paper: 11x14 Canson, Foundation Series, Red, White and black cover and can only be bought at Hobby Lobby for 8.00 but there is always a 40% discount coupon you can apply.

  • Pad of Bristol paper: 11x14 Strathmore or Canson, Vellum or Plate surface finish, 300 or 400 series.

  • Pentel “Clic” eraser found at all office supply stores

  • Kneaded eraser

  • Hand held or battery operated pencil sharpener

  • Roll of Scotch brand “Removable” tape mounted on a Blue Plaid card

  • Roll of white 1/2 inch “Artists Tape” available at Utrecht/Blick store

  • A makeup or soft basting brush to sweep away eraser particles

  • Tortillions – rolled paper smudge sticks (not needed for 1st class)

  • A good book to purchase: How to Draw Plants, by Keith West

I will have other drawing books for you to look through. If you have questions please e-mail me at ehult@cox.net
