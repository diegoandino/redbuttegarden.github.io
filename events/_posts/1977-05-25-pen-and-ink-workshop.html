REGISTRATION REQUIRED



FRIDAYS, MARCH 27 - APRIL 24 @ 6-9PM


This is a foundation class that will introduce students to nibs and holders as well as drafting and fiber tipped pens. A selection of inks will be available to the artist. Artists will complete creative pen and ink exercises and a personal mandala (circular design).


MATERIALS LIST:

Dinky Dips (4 containers in stand for 4 inks), Sketching nibs and holders, sponge, plastic box and try to hold materials will be provided for each artist.

  1. A pad of tracing paper (Canson Calique) red, white and black cover is the most translucent) only available locally at Hobby Lobby – 40% off coupon. Can be ordered from Dick Blick online.
  2. A roll of Scotch brand removable tape Purple plaid card.
  3. Three to four fiber-tipped drawing pens. Recommended brands can be purchased at Blick/Utrecht in Sugarhouse.
  4. Staedler Pigment Liner: best, archival, waterproof, lightfast and longer lasting. Best buy is a set of four pens in case, fine line to medium thick. Price: $13.15 locally. You can also buy individual pens for $3.59 to add to your collection.
  5. Three to four Micron Pigma Archival Pens, waterproof, fade-proof and is also available at Blick Utrecht, but is not as long lasting but are my second choice because they too are available locally, sets available. Buy 005, .01, .02 and .05.
    OR A set of three point sizes, Black — One each of 01 (0.25 mm), 03 (0.35 mm), and 05 (0.45 mm). Black ink. ADD the 005 which is not included in set price of $6.59. Individual pens cost: $2.59
  6. Pencils: 2H, 4H, and a Pentel Clic Eraser: a white plastic retractable eraser sold at Staples & OfficeMax and a kneaded gray eraser (widely available).
  7. 11"x14" Bristol 300 Series “Smooth” or Plate" finish paper pad. You might already have this paper. If not: Strathmore 300 Series pad with SMOOTH finish. This is an excellent paper for exercises.

NOTE: The above materials will be used again in Pen and Ink 2 and Scientific Illustration 1
