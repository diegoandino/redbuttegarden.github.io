REGISTRATION REQUIRED

This class is an extension of Pen & Ink 1, which is a prerequisite for Pen & Ink 2. Scientific pen strokes will be reviewed and applied to botanical drawings and a creative personal project. This class is required to take Scientific Illustration 1.



Three Day Workshop

THURSDAY, JANUARY 10, 6-9PM
FRIDAY, JANUARY 11, 6-9PM
SATURDAY, JANUARY 12, 9AM-4PM



MATERIALS LIST:

  • A pad of tracing paper (Canson Calco red, white and black cover) only available locally at Hobby Lobby (40% off coupon) and possibly Blick in Sugarhouse
  • A roll of Scotch brand removable tape Purple plaid card
  • A 12” ruler
  • 3 to 4 fiber-tipped drawing pens. Recommended brands can be purchased at Blick/Utrecht in Sugarhouse
  • Staedler Pigment Liner: best, archival, waterproof, lightfast and longer lasting. The best buy is a set of four pens in case, fine line to medium thick. Price: $13.15 locally. You can also buy individual pens for $3.59 to add to your collection
  • Micron Pigma archival, waterproof, fade-proof and is also available at Blick Utrecht, but is not as long lasting. These are my 2nd choice because they too are available locally, sets available. Buy 005, .01, .02 and .05
    OR a set of three point sizes: Black — One each of 01 (0.25 mm), 03 (0.35 mm), and 05 (0.45 mm). Black ink. ADD the 005 which is not included in set price of $6.59. Individual pens cost: $2.59
  • Pencils: 2H, 4H, and a Pentel Clic Eraser (a white plastic retractable eraser sold at Staples & Office Max) and a kneaded gray eraser (wildly available)
  • 11x14 Bristol 300 Series “Smooth” paper pad. You might already have this paper
  • Bristol pad. Smooth or Plate finish. This is an excellent paper for finished plates
