REGISTRATION REQUIRED

This class is an extension of Pen & Ink 1, where the penmanship of scientific illustration will be introduced.



Three Day Workshop

THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 16, 1-4PM
FRIDAY, FEBRUARY 17, 1-4PM
SATURDAY, FEBRUARY 18, 9AM-4PM



MATERIALS LIST:

Ink, Dinky Dips (for ink), Pen nibs and holders will be provided for each artist.

  1. A pad of tracing paper (Canson Calco red, white and black cover) only available locally at Hobby Lobby – 40% off coupon.

  2. A roll of Scotch brand removable tape Purple plaid card.

  3. A 12” ruler

  4. A ruling pen, Google “ruling pen” to see photos. I have seen them at Office supply stores that still carry drafting tools i.e. Staples, Office Max. Do not pay more than $6-$7 for it. Professional models are priced too high at $18, an inexpensive brush to fill with ink. Ruling pens used with a ruler make clean straight lines.

  5. Three to four fiber-tipped drawing pens. Recommended brands can be purchased at Blick/Utrecht in Sugarhouse.

  6. Staedler Pigment Liner: best, archival, waterproof, lightfast and longer lasting. The best buy is a set of four pens in case, fine line to medium thick. Price: $13.15 locally. You can also buy individual pens for $3.59 to add to your collection.

  7. Three to four Micron Pigma archival, waterproof, fade-proof also available at Blick Utrecht, but is not as long lasting but are my 2nd choice because they too are available locally, sets available. Buy 005, .01, .02 and .05.
    OR a set of three point sizes: Black — One each of 01 (0.25 mm), 03 (0.35 mm), and 05 (0.45 mm). Black ink. ADD the 005 which is not included in set price of $6.59. Individual pens cost: $2.59

  8. Pencils: 2H, 4H, and a Pentel Clic Eraser (a white plastic retractable eraser sold at Staples & Office Max) and a kneaded gray eraser (wildly available).

  9. 11x14 Bristol 300 Series “Smooth” paper pad. You might already have this paper. If not: Strathmore 300 Series

  10. Bristol pad. Smooth finish. This is an excellent paper for exercises.
