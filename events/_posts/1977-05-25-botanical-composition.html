Imaginative, interesting and dynamic composition can transform a botanical painting or illustration into a masterpiece. It is through composition that you introduce your own personal style into your work. This class helps to explain the elements that go towards creating a good composition and the basic principles underlying a successful illustration. The class will work entirely in graphite pencil, but the principles presented apply to all art media.

Materials List

  1. Graphite Pencils: 6H, 4H, 2H, HB, 2B, 2B, 4B,6B Brand of your choice. Tom Bow brand available only online

  2. Pad of Drawing Paper 11x14 Strathmore recommended available at Blick,Sugarhouse and Michaels 300 series or 400 series.

  3. Pad of tracing paper 11x14 Canson Foundation series, Red White and Black cover and can only be purchased locally at Hobby Lobby for around $8. There is always a 40% discount offered and can be accessed online at cashier.

  4. Pad of Bristol paper 11x14 Strathmore or Canson, Vellum or plate surface finish, 300 or 400 series

  5. Pentel white “Clic” eraser, refillable and available at all office supply stores

  6. A roll of “Scotch” brand “Removable” tape found in office supply stores on a blue plaid cardboard hanger hanging on a tape display

  7. A “kneaded” eraser It is gray and square shaped

  8. A hand held or battery operated pencil sharpener

  9. A roll of white “Artists’ Tape” available at Blick store in Sugarhouse

  10. A make-up or soft basring brush to sweep away eraser particles

  11. Small tortillions-rolled paper smudge sticks also available at Blick store
