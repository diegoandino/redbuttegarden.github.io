NOTE: WATERCOLOR 1 OR BOTH WATERCOLOR 1 & 2 ARE REQUIRED BEFORE REGISTERING

Watercolor-3 will introduce and revisit three watercolor techniques over a six-week class period, six hours per technique. We will work on hot or soft press watercolor paper (8x10”) and we will work from floral photos in watercolor combined with graphite, color pencil and pen and ink. This class will help prepare artists for the Rare & Endangered Plant classes in 2018 in preparation for an exhibition of such plants at the Red Butte Garden gallery, date not scheduled.

The three mini classes will include:
  • Watercolor & Graphite, Nov 15 & 22, 6 - 9PM
  • Watercolor & Color Pencil, Nov 29 and Jan 3, 6 - 9PM (No classes in December with time to work on your art in your studio.)
  • Watercolor & Pen and Ink, Jan 10 & 17, 6 - 9PM (pen & nibs with experience, or fiber tipped drawing pens)

Materials List

Art Supplies Provided:

  • Foamboard easel
  • Soft Press Watercolor paper
  • Arches Hot Press Watercolor paper
  • Lock-tite eraser
  • Portfolio with information - floral photos

Artist/Student Will Provide:

  • Watercolors: Your watercolor collection from previous classes and your palettes and watercolor accessories.
  • Brushes: Watercolor brushes you have, plus a "detail brush" #2, available at Dick Blick online.
  • Pencils: 4H, 3H, 2H, HB, H (if you have it), 2B. Color Pencils: Those you have already. Buy Prisma 24 if you need to buy.
  • Pens: Nibs and Pen holder you have already and have experience with or fiber tipped pens – Staedler pigment liners, Micron etc. sets have assorted sizes.
  • Tracing Paper: Canson in red and black pad. Most translucent and available at Hobby Lobby with 40% off.
  • Erasers: Pentel “Clic” eraser, refillable and retractable white pen-like eraser available at office Max, Staples, etc. Kneaded eraser: gray colored square at Blicks in Sugarhouse.
  • Tapes: Artists white tape 1/2”, and Scotch brand Removable tape with a BLUE Plaid backing hanging on a hook. Must be labeled REMOVABLE tape.
  • Papers: Pad of Drawing Paper 9"x12", If you have a preference for a particular WC paper not cold press, please bring to class, 8"x10".
  • Ruler and Ruling Pen if you have one.
